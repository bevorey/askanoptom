Cornea Open ✦ AI Synthesized
34M, unilateral progressive corneal thinning — KC or pellucid marginal degeneration?
Posted 3h ago 14 comments Dr. B. Reyaz, OD · Trinidad
Chief Complaint
Patient reports gradual blurring of right eye over 18 months. Contact lens wearer, recently unable to achieve adequate correction with soft lenses.
Findings
BCVA: 6/12 OD (with -4.50 -3.25 × 165), 6/6 OS. Slit lamp: mild inferior corneal thinning OD, no Fleischer ring visible, no Vogt's striae. Topography: inferior steepening, SimK 46.8 / 43.1 OD. Belin-Ambrosio D score: 3.2 OD. IOP normal OU.
Question for Discussion
Pattern suggests ectasia but location of thinning is more inferior-peripheral than classic KC apex. Considering KC vs PMD vs forme fruste. Would you proceed with CXL at this stage?
✦
AI Clinical Synthesis
Based on case details + 14 comments
Differential weighting: The topographic pattern with inferior peripheral thinning and a D-score of 3.2 is more consistent with pellucid marginal degeneration than classic KC, though forme fruste KC cannot be excluded without OD imaging. Commenters have noted the absence of a Fleischer ring argues against classic KC.

Consensus on CXL: 3 of 5 commenters recommend CXL referral now given documented progression over 18 months. Dr. K. Patel (UK) suggests Scheimpflug imaging to confirm pachymetry at thinnest point before proceeding. Sources: Rabinowitz 1998, DEWS Ectasia Guidelines 2019.

KP
Dr. K. Patel OD · London, UK 2h ago
The "crab claw" appearance on topography and the inferior-peripheral location of thinning strongly suggests PMD over KC. I'd get Scheimpflug before making any CXL decisions — if thinnest point is <400µm you're already limited on treatment options.

Agree with Dr. Patel. Also worth checking for systemic associations — atopy especially given the rubbing history. A D-score of 3.2 in a 34yo with documented progression is enough for me to refer for CXL evaluation regardless of KC vs PMD classification.
