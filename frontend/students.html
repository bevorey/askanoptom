slit lamp technique

Struggling with slit lamp illumination techniques — when do I use each one?

I get confused between diffuse illumination, direct focal, and indirect illumination. My supervisor keeps telling me to use indirect for corneal stromal lesions but I don't understand why. Can someone explain the logic?

BR
Dr. B. Reyaz OD · Supervising optometrist 1h ago

Great question. Think of it this way — direct focal illumination places the light and microscope focus on the same point, giving you maximum detail on surface lesions (epithelial defects, keratic precipitates). Indirect illumination displaces the beam slightly to one side so the lesion is lit from the side — this makes stromal lesions scatter light and become visible. It's the same principle as why dust in a room is only visible when light shines through it at an angle.

MJ
Marcus J. Final year · UWI optometry 45m ago

Also worth knowing — retroillumination (using the fundal reflex as your light source) is the one to use for lens opacities and corneal scars. Made a massive difference in my OSCE when I stopped trying to use direct illumination for cataracts.
